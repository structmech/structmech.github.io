EXERCISES IN EUCLIDEAN SPACE AND LINEAR MAPPINGS

Exercise 1: Verify that given \((a_1, a_2, …, a_n)\in\mathbb{R}^n\), the set of all \((x_1, x_2, ..., x_n)\in \mathbb{R}^n\) such that \(a_1x_1+a_2x_2+ … + a_nx_n=0\) is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^n\).

Solution: Let \(S\) be the set of all \((x_1, x_2, ..., x_n)\in \mathbb{R}^n\) such that \(a_1x_1+a_2x_2+ … + a_nx_n=0\). For any \(\mathbf{v}\in S\), \(\mathbf{v}\in\mathbb{R}^n\) therefore \(S\subset \mathbb{R}^n\).

Let \(\mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w}\) be any two vectors in \(S\). Then \(\mathbf{v}+\mathbf{w} = (v_1+w_1, v_2+w_2, …, v_n+w_n)\). It follows that \begin{equation*}(a_1(v_1+w_1) +a_2(v_2+w_2)+ … +a_n(v_n+w_n)) =(a_1v_1+a_2v_2+ … +a_nv_n+a_1w_1+a_2w_2+…+a_nw_n)\end{equation*} Because \(\mathbf{v}\in S, a_1v_1+a_2v_2+…+a_nv_n=0\) and because \(\mathbf{w}\in S, a_1w_1+a_2w_2+…+a_nw_n=0\). Therefore, \( (a_1(v_1+w_1) + a_2 (v_2+w_2) + … +a_n(v_n+w_n)) = 0 \quad \) which means that \(\mathbf{v}+\mathbf{w}\in S\). Therefore, \(S\) is closed under the operation of vector addition.

Let \(\alpha \in\mathbb{R}\) be any real number. Then, \(\alpha\mathbf{v}=(\alpha v_1, \alpha v_2, …, \alpha v_n)\) and \(a_1\alpha v_1 +a_2 \alpha v_2 + … +a_n \alpha v_n =\alpha(a_1v_1+a_2v_2+…+a_nv_n)=0\). This shows that \(\alpha \mathbf{v}\in S\) and \(S\) is closed under the operation of multiplication by a scalar.

Because \(S\) is a subset of \(\mathbb{R}^n\) and it is closed under the operations of vector addition and multiplication by a scalar, we conclude that \(S\) is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^n\).

Exercise 2: Prove that the intersection of two subspaces of \(\mathbb{R}^n\) is also a subspace.

Solution: Let \(S_1,S_2\) be subspaces of \(\mathbb{R}^n\) and let \(S=S_1\cap S_2\). For any \(\mathbf{v}\in S\), \(\mathbf{v}\in \mathbb{R}^n\), therefore \(S\) is a subset of \(\mathbb{R}^n\).

For any \(\mathbf{v},\mathbf{w}\in S\), \(\mathbf{v},\mathbf{w}\in S_1\) and \(\mathbf{v},\mathbf{w}\in S_2\). Since \(S_1\) and \(S_2\) are both subspaces, \(\mathbf{v}+\mathbf{w}\in S_1\) and \(\mathbf{v}+\mathbf{w}\in S_2\) which means that \(\mathbf{v}+\mathbf{w}\in S\). Therefore, \(S\) is closed under the operation of vector addition.

Let \(\alpha\in\mathbb{R}\) be any real number. Because \(\mathbf{v}\in S_1\), \(\mathbf{v}\in S_2\) and \(S_1,S_2\) are closed under the operation of multiplication by a scalar, \(\alpha v\in S_1\) and \(\alpha v\in S_2\). It follows that \(\alpha v\in S\) and \(S\) is closed under the operation of multiplication by a scalar.

We conclude that since \(S\) is a subset of \(\mathbb{R}^n\) and \(S\) is closed under the operations of vector addition and multiplication by a scalar, \(S\) is a subspace.

Exercise 3: Let \(V\) and \(W\) be subspaces of \(\mathbb{R}^n\), denote by \(V+W\) the following set: \begin{equation*}V+W=\lbrace \mathbf{v+w}:\mathbf{v}\in V \text{ and }\mathbf{w}\in W \rbrace\end{equation*} Show that \(V+W\) is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^n\).

Solution: Let \(\mathbf{v_0}+\mathbf{w_0}\in V+W\) with \(\mathbf{v_0}\in V\) and \(\mathbf{w_0}\in W\). Since \(V\) and \(W\) are subspaces of \(\mathbb{R}^n\), \(\mathbf{v_0}\in\mathbb{R}^n\), \(\mathbf{w_0}\in\mathbb{R}^n\) and \(\mathbf{v_0+w_0}\in\mathbb{R}^n\) and \(V+W\subset \mathbb{R}^n\).

Let \(\mathbf{v_1}+\mathbf{w_1}\in V+W\) with \(\mathbf{v_1}\in V\) and \(\mathbf{w_1}\in W\). Then \(\mathbf{v_0} + \mathbf{w_0}+\mathbf{v_1}+\mathbf{w_1}=(\mathbf{v_0}+\mathbf{v_1})+(\mathbf{w_0}+\mathbf{w_1})\). Because \(V\) and \(W\) are subspaces of \(\mathbb{R}^n\), they are closed under vector addition. It follows that, \(\mathbf{v_0}+\mathbf{v_1}\in V,\) \(\mathbf{w_0}+\mathbf{w_1}\in W,\) \(\mathbf{v_0}+\mathbf{w_0}+\mathbf{v_1}+\mathbf{w_1}\in V+W\) and \(V+W\) is closed under vector addition.

Let \(\alpha\in\mathbb{R}\). Then \(\alpha(\mathbf{v_0}+\mathbf{w_0})=\alpha \mathbf{v_0}+\alpha\mathbf{w_0}\). Since \(V, W\) are closed under multiplication by a scalar, \(\alpha \mathbf{v_0}\in V\) and \(\alpha\mathbf{w_0}\in W\) which means that \(\alpha(\mathbf{v_0}+\mathbf{w_0})\in V+W\) and \(V+W\) is closed under multiplication by a scalar as well. Therefore, \(V+W\) is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^n\).
