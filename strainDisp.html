STRAIN DISPLACEMENT RELATIONSHIPS

The displacements of a point \((x,y,z)\) in a three dimensional body in \(x,y,z\) directions are denoted with the letters \(u,v,w\) respectively. Displacement is a function of position, so we can write \(u(x,y,z),v(x,y,z),w(x,y,z)\). We distinguish between normal strains due to the elongation or shortening of an element inside the body and the shearing strains due to the distortion of that same element. The normal strains are denoted with \(\epsilon_{xx},\epsilon_{yy},\epsilon_{zz}\) and the shearing strains are denoted with \(\epsilon_{xy},\epsilon_{yz},\epsilon_{xz}\). Fig. 1 shows the displacement of an infinitesimal body in \(x\) direction

Using the definition of strain, \(\epsilon_{xx}\) can be calculated as in Eq.\eqref{eq:epsxx} \begin{equation} \epsilon_{xx}=\lim_{\Delta x\to 0}\frac{[L_{x1}]-[L_{x0}]}{L_{x0}}\lim_{\Delta x\to 0}\frac{[x+\Delta x+u(x+\Delta x,y,z)-(x+u(x,y,z))]-[\Delta x]}{\Delta x}=\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} \label{eq:epsxx} \end{equation}

Similarly, we can also obtain \(\epsilon_{yy}=\displaystyle\frac{\partial v}{\partial y}\) and \(\epsilon_{zz}=\displaystyle\frac{\partial w}{\partial z}\)

The distribution of the normal stress along the height of the beam cross-section is shown in Fig. 2 where the stresses are in the elastic range. In fig. 2 \(N.A.\) denotes the neutral axis where the normal stress is zero.

The bending moment \(M_z\) that causes the stresses in Fig. 2 can be calculated as in Eq.\eqref{eq:Mz} where \(E\) is the modulus of elasticity and \(\epsilon_x\) is the normal strain. \begin{equation} M_z=-\int_A\sigma_x y dA=-E\int_A\epsilon_x y dA \label{eq:Mz} \end{equation}

The relationship between the normal strain \(\epsilon_x\) and the radius of curvature \(\rho\) is illustrated in Fig. 3 where the height of the beam is exaggerated.

In Fig. 3 the dashed line is shorter than the netral axis after bending due to the compressive strains above \(N.A.\) where \(y>0\). The length of a fiber along the beam's length is denoted with \(L_1\). The initial length of this fiber before bending is equal to \(L_0\) which is the length of the neutral axis where the normal strain is equal to zero. Using these lengths before and after bending, the normal strain be calculated as in Eq.\eqref{eq:epsx} \begin{equation} \epsilon_x=\frac{L_1-L_0}{L_0}=\frac{(\rho-y)\theta-\rho\theta}{\rho\theta}=-\frac{y}{\rho} \label{eq:epsx} \end{equation} Inserting Eq.\eqref{eq:afterNeglect} in Eq.\eqref{eq:epsx} and Eq.\eqref{eq:epsx} in Eq.\eqref{eq:Mz} we obtain Eq.\eqref{eq:Mz2} \begin{equation} M_z=-\int_A\sigma_x y dA=-E\int_A\Big(-\frac{y}{\rho}\Big) y dA=\frac{EI_z}{\rho}=EI_zv^{\prime\prime} \label{eq:Mz2} \end{equation}
